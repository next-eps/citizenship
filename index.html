Here are the pros of having a New Zealand Passport

Thinking about applying for citizenship?

 

The eventual goal of any permanent resident is to become a citizen. But if you are still weighing the pros and cons, let us make it a little easier for you. Here is some useful information:

 

New Zealand Passport

The advantage of being a New Zealand passport holder is not only better visa-free travel but it also entitles you to consular protection from New Zealand missions overseas.

Security in your status

Being a Naturalized New Zealand citizen means that your residency cannot be revoked for any crime committed. Whereas Permanent residents and Australian citizens are at risk of losing their status in New Zealand if they commit certain crimes.

Becoming a citizen is the only way to guarantee you have the right to remain in New Zealand, it also means you wonâ€™t have a limit on the duration of your stay should you need to live abroad for some time.

Immigration for family members

Generally, permanent residents and Australian citizens are expected to have full-time residency in New Zealand in order to apply for sponsorship, However New Zealand citizens can sponsor spouses for migration while living outside New Zealand.

Also see New_Zealand_Citizenship_by_Descent

 

Education

The New Zealand educational system has been ranked as seventh worldwide, Made up of primary, middle, and secondary educational system.

Citizenship allows you full access to economic rights and full access to educational scholarships and awards that are only reserved for citizens of New Zealand.

Property Rights

Becoming a New Zealand citizen means you also have the right to own certain categories of rural property.

Job Market

Most New Zealand jobs are open to permanent residents, but some, especially Government or senior-level positions, may require the applicant to be a New Zealand Citizen.

These jobs are among the most stable and well-paid jobs in New Zealand, as a citizen you will now be able to compete for these jobs as well as other jobs that may require a higher security clearance.
